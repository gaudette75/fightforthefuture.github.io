Oleo Script

Sub heading type used to highlight sections on a page such as the projects on the FFTF home page or the column title in sub-pages

Helvetica Bold

Main buttons text. used on the donation, sign up and act now buttons at 18pt size

FFTF - Colors

Red #EE4741

Accent color, must be used in contextual terms. Use with the heart symbol or the flame icon on active projects.

Yellow #FBC337

Primary color for showing active and important things. Must be used carefully to enhance the brand of Fight for the Future.

Green #19A96A

Green must be used strictly for hyperlinks. Hover state for all hyperlinks must be the darker shade of green #026600

Gray #202020

Background color on home page. This color must be used only as a background color and in strict context.

White #FFFFFF

White must be used as a background image in all subpages for easier readability and consistency.

Blue #4965A8

Blue color here is derived from the main image we have. This color must be only used to overlay images that reside below the FFTF logo

Tiffiniy Cheng

Tiffiniy is our co-founder and co-director. She started Open Congress, Miro, almost got the SAFE Act passed until Chris Dodd pulled a late night vote - her work is on art, culture and structural power issues.

Holmes Wilson

Holmes is a co-founder of Fight for the Future. He also co-founded Miro, Open Congress, and Amara, and ran online campaigns for the FSF.

Zak Rogoff

Zak is our campaign manager. As a climate change activist in engineering school, he decided that fighting for Internet rights was important to almost all progressive causes.

Ali Ali

Ali is our lead designer. Outside of FFTF He teaches web design and animation at the Rochester Institute of Technology.

Joshua Blount

Joshua (@stickwithjosh to his friends) is our developer, hacking away at our various websites and projects. He cares deeply about carbonated beverages, the internet and you, and he previously worked at Canonical on Ubuntu One.

Douglas Schatz

Douglas graduated from Hampshire College in 2011 after writing a senior thesis entitled, "Sampling Copyright: an examination of, and proposed solution to, the fight between digital sampling and American copyright law". He has been interning at Fight for the Future ever since.
